Product Sense Interview Preparation

Key Knowledge Points

  • User Funnel Analysis: Understanding user journeys (e.g., awareness -> acquisition -> activation -> retention -> revenue -> referral - AARRR metrics). Identifying drop-off points.
  • Key Metrics & KPIs: Ability to define, select, and prioritize metrics for various products/features. Understanding leading vs. lagging indicators, vanity vs. actionable metrics.
  • North Star Metric: Identifying the single most important metric for a product's long-term success.
  • Trade-offs: Recognizing and discussing the trade-offs between different product goals or metrics (e.g., engagement vs. revenue, short-term vs. long-term).
  • Experimentation (A/B Testing): Understanding how A/B tests are used to validate product hypotheses and measure impact. (Detailed A/B testing concepts are in a separate page).
  • Problem Framing: Clearly defining a problem, its scope, and potential solutions.
  • Root Cause Analysis: Investigating why a metric changed (e.g., drop in user engagement).
  • Feature Prioritization: Methods for deciding which features to build next (e.g., RICE, ICE, MoSCoW).
  • Understanding User Needs: Empathizing with users, identifying pain points, and thinking from a user-centric perspective.
  • Business Acumen: Understanding the business model, competitive landscape, and strategic goals of the company.

Preparation Details

  • Practice Case Studies: Work through various product sense case studies (e.g., "Diagnose a drop in Instagram likes," "How would you measure success of a new feature on Google Maps?").
  • Structured Thinking: Develop a framework for approaching product sense questions (e.g., clarifying, identifying goals, brainstorming metrics, exploring causes, proposing solutions).
  • Read Product Blogs: Follow blogs from product leaders at major tech companies to understand current trends and thinking.
  • Think Critically about Products: Analyze products you use daily. How do they make money? What are their key metrics? How could they be improved?

Examples

Example 1: Measuring Success of a New Feature

Question: How would you measure the success of a new "Stories" feature on LinkedIn?

Answer Points:

  • Goal: Increase engagement and content creation on LinkedIn, potentially attracting a younger demographic or new types of content.
  • North Star Metric: Potentially "Total time spent on LinkedIn" or "Number of meaningful connections made/maintained."
  • Key Metrics:
    • Adoption: Number/percentage of active users who view/create stories.
    • Engagement: Daily/Weekly Active Story Viewers/Creators, average stories viewed per session, average stories created per user, completion rate of stories.
    • Retention: Retention of users who engage with Stories vs. those who don't.
    • Content Quality: User feedback, shares, comments on stories (qualitative).
  • Guardrail Metrics: Ensure the new feature doesn't cannibalize core product usage (e.g., time spent on feed, profile views) or negatively impact other key metrics.

Example 2: Diagnosing a Drop in User Engagement

Question: You notice a 10% drop in daily active users (DAU) for your mobile gaming app over the past week. How would you investigate this?

Answer Points:

  • Clarify: Is it a sudden drop or a gradual decline? Is it across all users/platforms/regions?
  • Hypotheses Generation (Framework: DRIVERS):
    • Data issue: Tracking error, data pipeline issue.
    • Release/Bug: New feature release, bug introduced.
    • Internal changes: Marketing campaign ended, internal policy change.
    • Volume: Fewer new users, less marketing spend.
    • External factors: Competitor launch, holiday, news event.
    • Retention: Users are churning faster.
    • Segment: Is the drop concentrated in a specific user segment (e.g., new users, specific country, device)?
  • Investigation Steps:
    • Check data freshness and integrity.
    • Look at recent deployments/feature flags.
    • Segment DAU by new vs. returning users, platform, geography, acquisition channel.
    • Analyze key engagement metrics (time spent, sessions per user, key action completions) for affected segments.
    • Check external news, competitor activity, app store reviews.
